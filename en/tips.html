Tips from a pro :

Because chimneys are used most during winter, spring is the best time to obtain a sweep. This will also give you all of summer for repairs in case any issues were detected during inspection. Furthermore, it's not uncommon that replacement parts are out of stock between September and January (due to higher demand).

You may schedule a sweep in autumn but it might be more difficult. That's because the thick fog we experience during that season makes rooftops slippery in the morning and thus workdays are shorter.

What types of firewood should I use?

  • All types, as long as they are dry! Here are the best ones in order of BTU (highest efficiency first): oak, maple, ash, birch and aspen (poplar).
  • If a softwood is used (fir, pine, spruce, cedar, larch or hemlock), you must set a spark screen before the fireplace.
  • Never use treated lumber for firewood!
  • Any wood that has been painted, varnished or stained is not recommended.
